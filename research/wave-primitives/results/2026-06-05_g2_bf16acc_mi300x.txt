Strategy                Median       Throughput
bf16 (fp32 acc)         1123.0 µs    0.058 Ge/s
bf16 (naive bf16)       2936.0 µs    0.022 Ge/s

  Overhead: -61.7% (fp32-acc is much FASTER than native bf16).

CONFIRMED, even more strongly than the __half case (H3 was -10.1%).
On gfx942 the naive-bf16 path round-trips through float on every reduction step
(no native bf16 add), making it expensive. Promoting bf16 -> float32 once and
reducing in float is strictly superior: lower latency AND higher accuracy.

This extends H3 (fp32-accumulation is the right default for low-precision wave
reductions on CDNA3) from __half to bf16. For both half types, the library's
fp32-accumulation choice is the correct one — not a precision/speed tradeoff but
a win on both axes.
